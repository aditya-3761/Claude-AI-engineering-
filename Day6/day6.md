What changed and why it raised the score:

Added a Professional Summary — ATS systems weigh the top third of a resume heavily; a keyword-dense summary immediately signals relevance to engineering/ML roles.
Removed icons, columns, and symbols — the original used icons (📍📞 #§ï) and a multi-column layout, which most ATS parsers either misread or skip entirely.
Rewrote all bullet points with action verbs — changed passive descriptions ("Developed a… with") to impact-first phrasing ("Engineered," "Architected," "Integrated") which recruiters scan for.
Expanded skill keyword coverage — explicitly named "JWT Authentication," "REST APIs," "Hugging Face Transformers," "RBAC," and "CI/CD" as standalone parseable terms rather than burying them in project descriptions only.
Standardized section headings — replaced custom labels with ATS-standard headings (Education, Projects, Technical Skills, Achievements) that every major ATS recognizes.
Structured projects with tech stack on the same line as title — ATS parsers look for skill-to-project associations; co-locating them on one line improves keyword-to-context matching.
Removed emoji and special Unicode characters — characters like §, ï, and # cause parser errors in Workday, Greenhouse, and Lever, potentially dropping your resume entirely.
Plain-text contact line — original had unresolved placeholder links; the optimized version presents contact info as clean, parseable text.
